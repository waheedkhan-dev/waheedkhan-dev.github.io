Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 26, 2026

Routinic is a habit tracking application for Android. Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how Routinic handles data and information when you use the app. Routinic is designed to work primarily offline and does not require user registration or an account.

1. Information We Collect

Routinic is designed to work primarily offline and does not require user registration.

We do not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, phone number, or location.

However, the app may collect limited anonymous data through third-party services to improve app performance and stability:

  • Habit data: Habits you create, schedules, completion records, streaks, and Awesome Day history — stored locally on your device only.
  • Timer session data: Duration and completion state of timed habits — stored locally on your device only.
  • App preferences: Your chosen theme (Light, Dark, Dark Blue), reminder times, and settings — stored locally on your device only.
  • Ad consent choice: Whether you accept or decline personalised advertising via Google's UMP at first launch — stored on your device and shared with AdMob as required by their platform.
  • Crash and diagnostic data: Anonymous crash reports and app performance data collected via Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Analytics to help us identify and fix issues.

2. Local Data Storage

All habit data, progress logs, streaks, and schedules are stored locally on your device. We do not upload, store, or process your habit data on external servers. You retain full ownership and control over your data.

  • The app works fully without an internet connection.
  • If you uninstall Routinic or clear the app's data, your information is permanently deleted. We cannot recover it.
  • We recommend enabling Android's automatic device backup to protect against accidental loss.

3. How We Use Your Information

Since your personal habit data stays on your device, it is used solely to power the app's features:

  • Displaying your habits, completion history, and streaks on screen
  • Calculating statistics, bar-chart analytics, calendar heatmap, and Awesome Days
  • Firing local reminders and alarms at the times you configure
  • Running the built-in countdown timer for timed habits
  • Applying your selected theme and display preferences
  • Diagnosing crashes and improving app stability via Firebase Crashlytics
  • Understanding general app usage patterns via Firebase Analytics to improve features

We do not use your data for profiling, targeted marketing, or any purpose beyond running and improving the app.

4. Analytics and Crash Reporting

Routinic uses the following third-party services for crash reporting and analytics:

  • Firebase Crashlytics
  • Firebase Analytics

These services may collect anonymous information such as:

  • Device model and operating system version
  • App usage statistics and session duration
  • Crash logs and diagnostics
  • Screen views and feature interaction counts

This data is used only to improve app performance, fix bugs, and understand feature usage. It does not include your habit content and is not used to identify you personally. See Firebase's Privacy Policy for details.

5. Advertising (Google AdMob)

Routinic is free to download and use. To support ongoing development, the app displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob.

Consent is requested at first launch. When you first open Routinic, Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) will ask whether you consent to personalised advertising. You may accept or decline. To change your choice later, go to your Android Google account settings under Ads & data, or reinstall the app to see the prompt again.

  • If you accept personalised ads: Google AdMob may use your device's advertising identifier (GAID) to show relevant ads, governed by Google's Privacy Policy.
  • If you decline personalised ads: You will still see ads, but not targeted ones. No advertising identifier is used for targeting.
  • Routinic does not receive or store any data from the ad network.

Review Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent user data.

We only share limited anonymous data with third-party services used for:

  • Analytics and crash reporting (Firebase)
  • Advertising (Google AdMob)

These services receive only the anonymised data described above. Your personal habit data is never shared.

7. App Permissions

Routinic requests only the permissions it genuinely needs:

PermissionNameWhy it is needed
POST_NOTIFICATIONSNotificationsTo send habit reminders at your chosen times. Disable anytime in Android Settings → Apps → Routinic → Notifications.
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARMExact AlarmsTo fire reminders at the precise time you set, including across DST changes. Required on Android 12+.
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETEDBoot ReceiverTo reschedule your reminders automatically after your device restarts.
INTERNETInternetRequired by AdMob to load ads and by Firebase to send crash and analytics reports. Not used to transmit your habit data.
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATENetwork StateUsed by AdMob to check connectivity before loading ads.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

Since all habit data is stored locally on your device:

  • Uninstalling the app will permanently delete all app data.
  • Clearing app data via Android Settings → Apps → Routinic → Storage will also permanently delete your data.

We do not store user habit data on our servers and therefore have nothing to delete on our end.

9. Children's Privacy

Routinic is not specifically directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because no account is required and all data is stored locally, no personal information is collected from any user regardless of age.

10. Your Rights and Choices

  • Access your data: All data is visible directly within the app.
  • Delete your data: Delete habits in the app, or go to Android Settings → Apps → Routinic → Storage → Clear data.
  • Change your ad consent: Go to your Android Google account settings under Ads & data, or reinstall the app.
  • Disable notifications: Android Settings → Apps → Routinic → Notifications.
  • Opt out of personalised ads globally: Android Settings → Google → Ads → Delete advertising ID.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updates will be reflected by the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document.

Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.
